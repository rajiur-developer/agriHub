Pest Control Strategies

Integrated Pest Management (IPM) for sustainable crop protection.

1

Monitoring & Early Detection

The foundation of IPM is regular observation. Early detection allows for milder treatments.

  • Field Scouting: Walk fields weekly in a 'W' pattern to check for eggs or damage.
  • Traps: Use yellow sticky traps for aphids and pheromone traps for moths.
  • Identify: Distinguish between pests and beneficial insects.
2

Biological Controls

Use natural enemies to suppress pest populations without chemicals.

  • Predators: Release Ladybugs to eat aphids and Lacewings for soft-bodied insects.
  • Parasitoids: Use Trichogramma wasps to parasitize caterpillar eggs.
  • Birds: Install birdhouses to attract insect-eating birds.
3

Organic Remedies

If pests exceed economic thresholds, use organic interventions.

  • Neem Oil: Disrupts the life cycle of pests like whiteflies and beetles.
  • Insecticidal Soap: Effective contact killer for soft-bodied pests.
  • Diatomaceous Earth: A powder that dehydrates crawling insects like slugs.
Pro Tip: Always start with cultural controls (Crop Rotation, Sanitation, and Healthy Soil) before moving to biological or organic remedies.
