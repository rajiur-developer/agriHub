Comprehensive Farming Guides

Expert methodologies, seasonal tips, and crop-specific tutorials.

Building healthy soil is the foundation of organic farming. Follow these steps to create nutrient-rich soil without synthetic fertilizers.

Step-by-Step Process:
  • 1 Test Your Soil: Use a pH kit to determine acidity levels. Most vegetables prefer pH 6.0-7.0.
  • 2 Aerate: Gently turn the soil using a broadfork to improve oxygen flow without destroying fungal networks.
  • 3 Add Organic Matter: Spread 2-3 inches of compost or aged manure across the topsoil.
  • 4 Mulch: Cover with straw or leaf mold to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
Tip: Avoid tilling too deep as it brings dormant weed seeds to the surface.

A guide to soil-less farming using the Deep Water Culture (DWC) method.

Requirements:
  • Reservoir container (opaque)
  • Net pots and clay pebbles
  • Air pump and air stone
  • Nutrient solution
Setup Instructions:
  1. Fill reservoir with water and add nutrients according to package instructions.
  2. Place air stone in water to oxygenate roots.
  3. Place plants in net pots with clay pebbles for support.
  4. Ensure roots touch the water, but the stem stays dry to prevent rot.

Spring Planting

Spring is the busiest time for preparation. Focus on cool-weather crops.

  • Start seedlings indoors (Tomatoes, Peppers).
  • Direct sow peas, spinach, and radishes as soon as soil thaws.
  • Prune fruit trees before buds break.

Winter Maintenance

Protecting your soil and equipment during the dormant season.

  • Plant cover crops (Clover, Rye) to fix nitrogen.
  • Drain irrigation hoses to prevent freezing.
  • Service machinery (Oil change, sharpen blades).

Land Preparation & Sowing

Wheat requires a well-pulverized seedbed. Plow the land 2-3 times followed by planking.

Key Stages:
  1. Sowing: Use seed drills for uniform depth (4-5cm).
  2. Irrigation: Critical at Crown Root Initiation (CRI) stage (21 days after sowing).
  3. Harvesting: Harvest when grains are hard and moisture is below 14%.

Indeterminate tomatoes require regular maintenance for maximum yield.

Best Practices:
  • Suckering: Remove the shoots that grow in the "armpit" between the main stem and branches.
  • Staking: Use cages or a trellis system. Tie the main stem loosely every 6-8 inches.
  • Watering: Water at the base to keep leaves dry and prevent blight.

Pest Control Strategies

Integrated Pest Management (IPM) for healthy crops.

Biological control: Ladybugs eating aphids.

Monitoring & Detection

Regular scouting of fields is the first line of defense. Use pheromone traps to detect insect populations early before they cause economic damage.

Biological Control

Encourage natural predators. For example, introducing parasitic wasps or ladybugs can naturally reduce aphid and caterpillar populations without chemicals.

Organic Remedies

Use Neem oil, insecticidal soaps, or diatomaceous earth. These are effective against soft-bodied insects while causing minimal harm to the environment.
